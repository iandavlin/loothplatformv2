Effective date: May 23, 2026

The Looth Group is a community of luthiers, repair professionals, restorers, players, and enthusiasts. These Community Guidelines describe the standards of conduct that keep this community useful, civil, and welcoming. They supplement, and are part of, our Terms of Service. Violations may result in content removal, warnings, posting restrictions, suspension, or permanent account termination, in our sole discretion.

1. Be Respectful

  • Treat other members with courtesy, even in disagreement.
  • Critique work and ideas, not people.
  • Assume good faith. Ask before you accuse.

2. No Harassment or Intimidation

Harassment, bullying, doxxing, threats, sustained insults, or unwanted sexual attention are prohibited. This includes:

  • Personal attacks, slurs, or hate speech directed at a person or group based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or age;
  • Publishing or threatening to publish another member's private information (address, employer, family details) without consent;
  • Following a member across topics to disparage them;
  • Coordinated pile-ons or brigading.

3. No Illegal or Harmful Content

  • No content that violates law, including content that infringes intellectual-property rights, incites violence, or promotes illegal activity;
  • No sexually explicit material, content depicting minors in sexual contexts, or graphic violence;
  • No instructions or facilitation for activity that could cause serious physical harm.

4. Respect Intellectual Property

  • Post only content you created or have permission to share.
  • Credit sources. When you share another person's photos, diagrams, or writing, identify and link to the source.
  • If you believe content on the Services infringes your copyright, see our DMCA / Copyright Policy.

5. Keep Commerce Honest

The Looth Group supports member businesses, but commerce must be disclosed and honest:

  • If you are paid or compensated for endorsing a product, say so.
  • No misleading claims about your services, qualifications, or the work of others.
  • No covert advertising or astroturfing.
  • Solicitations to take business off-platform must comply with any explicit rules in the relevant forum or group.

6. No Spam

  • Do not post the same content repeatedly across topics or threads;
  • Do not flood the site with low-effort posts to inflate activity;
  • Do not use the Services to send unsolicited bulk messages;
  • Do not register multiple accounts to evade moderation.

7. Stay On Topic

Within each forum, group, or thread, contribute content relevant to that space. Off-topic material may be moved or removed.

8. Use Real Identities Honestly

You may use a screen name, but you may not impersonate another person, misrepresent your professional credentials, or pretend to be a Looth Group employee or moderator.

9. Respect Privacy

  • Do not share another member's private messages, photos, or personal information without their permission.
  • If you photograph another person's instrument or workspace, get their consent before posting.

10. Moderation

Moderators and the Looth Group team may, at our sole discretion:

  • Edit, hide, move, or remove content;
  • Lock or close threads;
  • Issue warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans;
  • Restrict posting privileges, including in specific forums.

We do not pre-screen content. Moderation actions are typically triggered by member reports or by routine review.

Repeated or severe violations may result in immediate permanent account termination without refund. Account terminations are final unless you successfully appeal at ian.davlin@gmail.com within thirty (30) days of termination.

11. Reporting

If you encounter content or behavior that violates these Guidelines, use the in-product report feature where available, or email ian.davlin@gmail.com with:

  • A link to the content or thread;
  • A brief description of the issue; and
  • Any supporting context.

Do not retaliate against the member you are reporting. Retaliation is itself a violation of these Guidelines.

12. Changes to These Guidelines

We may revise these Guidelines as the community evolves. Material changes will be communicated by reasonable means.
